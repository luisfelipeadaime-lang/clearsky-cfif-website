About CFIF

What is CFIF?

CFIF is an industry-led Emissions Reduction/Fuel Production (ERFP) fund created specifically for primary fuel suppliers in Canada. It provides a simple, compliant pathway for fuel suppliers to meet their obligations under the Clean Fuel Regulations (CFR).

Instead of navigating complex project development or bilateral credit markets individually, participating fuel suppliers pool contributions into a professionally managed fund. CFIF then invests in a diversified portfolio of emissions-reduction and clean fuel projects across Canada.

The result is predictable compliance costs, reduced administrative burden, and meaningful contribution to Canada's clean energy transition. CFIF is governed by the industry it serves, ensuring alignment between regulatory obligations and investment decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

CFIF Inc. is a Canadian not-for-profit corporation and industry-led ERFP fund under Canada's Clean Fuel Regulations. All contributions are deployed as non-repayable grants to eligible clean fuel projects. CFIF does not seek financial returns.

CFIF is designed for primary fuel suppliers subject to obligations under Canada's Clean Fuel Regulations. Any registered primary supplier seeking a compliant, efficient pathway to meet CFR requirements may be eligible to contribute.

CFIF invests in a diversified portfolio of eligible projects including emissions-reduction initiatives, low-carbon fuel production facilities, and enabling infrastructure. All funded projects must meet the eligibility criteria established under the Clean Fuel Regulations.

CFIF is governed by its contributing members through an industry advisory committee. This ensures that investment decisions reflect the priorities, expertise, and interests of the fuel supply sector. An independent fund administrator provides operational oversight and regulatory compliance.

Contribution decisions can be made throughout the compliance year, giving you strategic flexibility to act based on current market conditions and your compliance position.
