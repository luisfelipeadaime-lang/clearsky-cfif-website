Understanding the Clean Fuel Regulations

Canada's Clean Fuel Regulations (CFR) require primary fuel suppliers to progressively reduce the lifecycle carbon intensity of the liquid fossil fuels they produce, import, and distribute. These regulations apply to producers and importers of gasoline, diesel, and heating oil, establishing annual carbon intensity reduction targets that tighten each compliance year through 2030 and beyond.

To meet their obligations, regulated parties have three principal compliance pathways. They can undertake direct project investments in clean fuel production, low-carbon fuel blending, or upstream emissions reductions. They can purchase compliance credits on the open market from parties who have generated surplus reductions. Or they can make contributions to an Eligible Revenue-Positive Fund (ERFP) that invests in qualifying clean fuel projects on their behalf.

An ERFP fund is a pooled investment vehicle specifically designed under the Clean Fuel Regulations to aggregate contributions from multiple fuel suppliers and deploy that capital into a diversified portfolio of clean fuel projects. The fund must meet strict federal eligibility criteria: investments must target lifecycle carbon intensity reductions in Canadian transportation fuels, and the fund must operate with transparent governance, independent verification, and regular reporting to both contributors and the regulator. CFIF is structured as an ERFP fund, providing the simplest and most professionally managed compliance pathway available to Canadian fuel suppliers.

Predictable Compliance and Risk Management

For individual fuel suppliers, the compliance landscape presents significant uncertainty. Credit market prices fluctuate based on supply and demand dynamics, regulatory interpretation changes, and the pace at which competing project developers bring new credit supply online. A fuel supplier relying solely on credit purchases faces budget variability that can be difficult to plan around, particularly for smaller and mid-sized companies without dedicated regulatory teams.

Direct project investment, while potentially cost-effective over the long term, demands specialized expertise in clean fuel technology assessment, project development, construction oversight, and ongoing verification. Most fuel companies are experts in fuel logistics, refining, and distribution, not in evaluating the technical and financial viability of renewable natural gas facilities, hydrogen production plants, or electric vehicle charging infrastructure. The learning curve is steep and the capital risk is concentrated.

CFIF addresses both challenges. By pooling contributions from multiple fuel suppliers, the fund creates a diversified portfolio that spreads project risk across technologies, geographies, and development stages. Contributors benefit from a fixed, predictable contribution schedule that can be budgeted with confidence. Professional fund management brings the technical and financial expertise that individual companies would otherwise need to build internally. The result is lower per-unit compliance costs, reduced administrative burden, and significantly less exposure to any single project failing to deliver expected reductions.

Industry-Led, Not Government-Adjacent

CFIF is governed by its contributing members. The fund's investment committee is composed of representatives from participating fuel suppliers, supplemented by independent clean energy and financial advisors. This structure ensures that investment decisions reflect the commercial realities, operational constraints, and strategic priorities of the companies whose capital is at work. Unlike government-administered programs, CFIF does not route capital through bureaucratic allocation processes or apply policy-driven filters that may not align with commercial viability.

Contributing members have a direct voice in shaping the fund's investment thesis. The investment committee meets quarterly to review the project pipeline, approve new investments, and assess portfolio performance. Members receive detailed reporting on each project's progress, carbon intensity reductions achieved, and financial returns generated. This level of transparency and participation gives fuel suppliers confidence that their compliance dollars are being deployed effectively and in alignment with their industry's needs.

The industry-led model also creates a platform for collective advocacy. As a unified group of fuel suppliers investing in clean fuel solutions, CFIF members carry significant weight in regulatory consultations, policy discussions, and industry forums. The fund provides a credible, evidence-backed voice that demonstrates the fuel supply sector's commitment to the clean energy transition while advocating for practical, workable regulatory frameworks.
