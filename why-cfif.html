The Regulation

Canada's Clean Fuel Regulations

The Clean Fuel Regulations (CFR) are Canada's landmark framework requiring primary fuel suppliers to progressively reduce the lifecycle carbon intensity of the liquid fossil fuels they produce, import, and distribute. The CFR applies to all producers and importers of gasoline, diesel, and heating oil.

CFR Key Facts

  • Applies to all primary suppliers of liquid fossil fuels in Canada
  • Annual carbon intensity reduction targets tightening through 2030 and beyond
  • Compliance credits can be generated or purchased on the open market
  • Contributions to an ERFP fund count as a compliance pathway (up to 10% of obligation)

Two Compliance Requirements

Under the CFR, primary fuel suppliers face two distinct compliance obligations:

1. Reduction Requirement

Fuel suppliers must reduce the carbon intensity of their fuel pool by a prescribed annual percentage. This is the core compliance obligation that drives demand for compliance credits.

2. ERFP Contribution Option

Fuel suppliers may satisfy up to 10% of their annual reduction requirement through contributions to an approved ERFP fund like CFIF, providing a simplified compliance pathway.

The Challenge

Open market compliance credit prices are volatile and rising. Credits currently trade at $395+/credit and have seen a 4X increase since the CFR took effect. Individual project development requires specialized expertise, significant capital, and carries concentrated risk. Most fuel suppliers are experts in logistics, refining, and distribution — not in evaluating renewable natural gas facilities or hydrogen production plants.
